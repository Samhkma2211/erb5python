Frequently Asked Questions

+ Q1. Why should I adopt a dog instead of buying from a breeder?
Adopting a dog saves a life, reduces demand for puppy mills, and is often more affordable. Shelters provide vaccinated, spayed/neutered dogs, and you’ll support their mission to help animals in need.
+ Q2. What are the typical requirements for adopting a dog?
Requirements vary but often include being at least 18–21 years old, proof of stable income, landlord approval (if renting), a home visit, references, and an application form. Adoption fees usually apply.
+ Q3. How much does dog adoption cost?
HKD 800-1000 typically covers vaccines, microchipping and spay/neuter. Budget HKD 500-1000/month for ongoing costs like food, vet visits, and grooming.
+ Q4. How do I choose the right dog for my lifestyle??
Consider your activity level, living space, and time availability. Puppies and high-energy breeds need more attention, while seniors or smaller dogs may suit quieter homes. Shelter staff can help match you!
+ Q5. What if the dog has behavioral issues?
Shelters often assess dogs’ behavior and disclose known issues. Many offer post-adoption support or training resources. Patience, consistency, and professional trainers can address most challenges.
+ Q6. Are adopted dogs healthy?
Shelters provide basic vet care, but health histories can be incomplete. Schedule a vet visit post-adoption to check for underlying issues and establish a care plan.
